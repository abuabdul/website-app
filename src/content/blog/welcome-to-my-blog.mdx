## Hello, world

After years of telling myself "I should start a blog", I finally did it. This is that blog.

I've been in software engineering for over a decade — building products, leading teams, migrating monoliths, arguing about tab widths (spaces, obviously). Over that time I've accumulated a lot of hard-won knowledge that lives mostly in my head, in Slack threads, and in half-finished Notion docs.

This blog is my attempt to get it out of those places and into a form that might be useful to someone.

## What to expect

I'll write about things I actually care about and have practical experience with:

- **Software architecture** — microservices, event-driven systems, domain modelling, and the trade-offs that don't fit in a tweet
- **Engineering leadership** — mentoring, technical strategy, code review culture, and what "senior" actually means
- **Career growth** — going from developer to principal engineer, navigating the IC-to-management question, getting better at communicating technical ideas
- **Tools and practices** — things I've tried, what worked, what didn't, and why

I'll try to be direct, specific, and honest about what I don't know. I'd rather write one useful post a month than ten vague ones a week.

## Who this is for

Mainly for developers who are a few years into their career and wondering what comes next. But honestly, I'm writing this for myself too — explaining something clearly is how I figure out whether I actually understand it.

If you find something useful here, let me know. If you think I'm wrong about something, I'd genuinely like to hear it.

See you in the posts.
